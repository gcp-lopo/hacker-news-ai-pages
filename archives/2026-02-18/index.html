AI tools already act as a force multiplier for adversaries , allowing them to delegate key steps in their campaigns, whether it be for conducting reconnaissance, vulnerability scanning, crafting convincing phishing emails, creating synthetic identities, debugging code, or developing malware. Viewed differently, this approach is no different from attack campaigns that have weaponized trusted services for malware distribution and C2.
